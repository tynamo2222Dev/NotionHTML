The Slave Trade and Africa → Chapter 13 (648-654)

Capturing & shipping enslaved people

  • The trade done in the New World and by European merchants wouldn’t be happening if it wasn’t for the slave trading networks in Africa
  • European slave traders took advantage of the political rivalries in the interior of Africa to get on the good sides of African elites, and getting slaves in the process
  • Though prior to the Europeans, there was already a slave trading system in Africa, consisting of slaves going from the Sahara to North Africa, to the Red Sea and Swahili Coast of Africa → Though the amount of slaves was nothing compared to the trading of slaves done by Europeans
  • Many slaves were held in camps, awaiting their transportation to the New World. In these camps, some would die, the others were jammed onto boats and would wait there until the “Cargo” was full to depart to the New World. Many slaves would die in the process of getting to the New World

Slavery’s gender imbalance

  • European merchants generally preferred male labor over female labor, creating an imbalance in gender both in Africa (now being mostly women) and the Americas (now being mostly men)
  • Though in the African suppliers, female slaves were valued more because of their importance to the production of grain, leathers, and more
  • In Africa, the practice of polyamory also became more common because of the lack of men, but it kept the population up
